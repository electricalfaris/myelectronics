Quick Review

  1. Resistors:

    • Resistor is a common passive two-terminal electrical component that opposes the flow of current.

  2. Capacitors:

    • Capacitors store electrical energy in an electric field.

  3. Inductors:

    • Inductors store energy in a magnetic field when current flows through them.

  4. Diodes:

    • Diodes allow current to flow in one direction only.

  5. Transistors:

    • Transistors are semiconductor devices used to amplify or switch electronic signals and electrical power.

  6. Integrated Circuits (ICs):

    • Integrated circuits contain multiple electronic components (such as transistors, resistors, capacitors) fabricated on a single semiconductor chip.

  7. LEDs (Light Emitting Diodes):

    • LEDs emit light when current flows through them in the forward direction.

  8. Switches:

    • Switches are devices used to interrupt or divert the flow of electricity in a circuit.

  9. Potentiometers:

    • Potentiometers are variable resistors used to control electrical resistance in a circuit.

  10. Connectors:

    • Connectors are used to join electrical circuits together reliably and securely.
